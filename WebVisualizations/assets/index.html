EPL Predictive Metrics and Outcomes

This project looks at various predictive metrics in English top flight association football from the 2019-2020 season and compares those metrics with actual outcomes. Those metrics include:

  • xG: Expected goals, the aggregated chances of a shot's likelihood to become a goal based on historical predictive metrics, such as shot location, strong/weak foot use, and distance from goal.
  • npxG: Non-penalty expected goals, aggregates all non-penalty shots' likelihood of becoming a goal. Penalties are awarded an xG value of .75, as they are scored 75% of the time on average.
  • xGA: Expected goals against, the aggregated chances of the opposite team's expected goals.
  • xpts: Expected points, uses the previous metrics to predict how many points a team should get from a match. In association football, a win earns 3 points, a draw earns 1 point, and a loss results in 0 points.
  • ppda: Pressures per defensive action, the mean amount of attempted pressure actions commited to retrieve possession from the other team.
